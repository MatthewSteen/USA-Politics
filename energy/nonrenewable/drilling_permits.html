Drilling Permits#

Oil and gas drilling on Federal lands has become a divisive political issue in the context of gasoline prices, energy security, and climate change. When gasoline prices increase, there’s often renewed attention to increasing production from Federal lands. However, this solution ignores several facts. First, crude oil prices are determined by the global market[1]. Second, the majority of oil production in the US comes from private, not public, land[2] [3]. Third, although the US as a whole is a top petroleum producer, the percentage of global production from Federal lands is small because it’s a small percentage of total US production.

The purpose of this analysis is to compare the total number of approved drilling permits on Federal land during the 45th and 46th President Of The United States (POTUS) first term.

Methodology#

This analysis uses raw data from the Bureau of Land Management Approved Applications for Permits to Drill (APDs) Report[4] using the following steps.

  1. Set Date Limit Between (e.g. 01/20/2027 - 01/19/2020)

  2. Click Spreadsheet Listing tab

  3. Click Apply button and wait to download the .xls file

  4. Open .xls file and Save As .csv

# import python libraries
import pandas as pd

# paths to files

Conclusions#

The results above show that there have been 6% more drilling permits approved during Biden’s first three years (9963 total) than Trump’s first three years (9363 total) in office. These numbers align closely with another analysis of drilling permits approved on federal lands[2] using a similar data source.
